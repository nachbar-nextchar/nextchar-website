What Is Biochar?

Biochar is a nearly pure carbon, all-natural material produced by heating biomass in an oxygen-reduced environment. The result is a stable, low-ash, extremely porous compound that dramatically improves soil health, sequesters atmospheric carbon, and remediates contaminated land.

Unlike conventional soil amendments, biochar persists for hundreds to thousands of years — repairing poor and overworked soils in ways that last generations.

Applications

Agriculture
